<p style="text-align: justify;">
<em>Part III of "NCVR - Numerical Computing via Rust" turns to randomness and the ordering of data. It examines how pseudorandom numbers are generated and used, and the properties that make a generator suitable for numerical work, alongside the fundamental problems of sorting and selection. Though seemingly distinct, these subjects share a concern with structure and organization in data, and both are essential building blocks for simulation, statistics, and higher-level algorithms. The focus is on understanding these primitives clearly and implementing them reliably in Rust.</em>
