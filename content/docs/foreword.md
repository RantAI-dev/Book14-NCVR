<p style="text-align: justify;">
"Numerical Computing via Rust (NCVR)" emerges at a time when numerical computing stands at the forefront of progress in both business and academia. From optimizing logistics and forecasting complex market behaviors to advancing high-performance simulations in physics and engineering, the power of numerical methods underpins transformative solutions in countless domains. My own journey began with established languages like FORTRAN, C/C++, Pascal, and Python, each representing vital steps toward ever more efficient and flexible programming ecosystems. Yet through these valuable experiences, it became clear that a new language was needed—one that could offer the robustness of low-level power while seamlessly meeting the nuanced requirements of modern computation.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
The moment has arrived, propelled by a new generation of Artificial Intelligence capable of converting mathematical concepts into working code with unprecedented accuracy. This surge of GenAI has sparked a profound shift: we now have the means to translate mathematical expressions and entire algorithms across languages such as Python, C/C++, and Rust in an instant. These capabilities open up a realm of possibilities, especially as the horizons of numerical computing expand into data-intensive fields like machine learning and artificial intelligence. In this evolving landscape, Rust has emerged as the prime candidate to propel numerical methods into a future defined by performance, parallelism, concurrency, and scalability. With its strong guarantees around memory safety and efficiency, Rust not only preserves the computational muscle expected by seasoned developers but also provides the stable foundation necessary for cutting-edge research and industrial applications.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
We can envision a world where Rust is as integral to operating systems and drivers as it is to complex simulation frameworks and AI libraries. Through the language's design, parallel computations become safer to implement, concurrency issues become more approachable, and the likelihood of runtime errors that hamper scientific discovery is drastically reduced. As AI continues its rapid ascension, placing even greater demands on processing power and reliability, it is only natural that Rust steps into the limelight, offering a path to harness increasingly powerful hardware without sacrificing clarity and maintainability.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
This book represents the culmination of a belief that classic numerical computing approaches can be revitalized for the modern age. Rather than relying on traditional FORTRAN or C/C++ implementations, we have rebuilt these timeless algorithms through Rust crates, forging a bridge between decades of proven numerical theory and the innovations of twenty-first-century computing. We plan to use these Rust implementations in academic courses, specialized research, and production systems, confident that this fresh approach will not only carry forward the legacy of established computational methods but also expand them into the next wave of scientific and technological breakthroughs.
</p>

<p style="text-align: justify;">
Our hope is that this work will empower researchers, students, and professionals with reliable numerical methods for years to come. With the book in your hands, we trust you will feel inspired to embark upon your own exploration of Rust's capabilities, confident that you are equipped with a language ready to meet the challenges of tomorrow's most demanding computational tasks.
</p>
