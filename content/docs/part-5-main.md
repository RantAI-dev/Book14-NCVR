<em>Part V of "NCVR - Numerical Computing via Rust" explores spectral methods and the transforms that power them. It introduces the Fast Fourier Transform as a cornerstone algorithm and then surveys the broad range of Fourier and spectral applications that build upon it. Working in the frequency domain offers a powerful complement to direct computation, enabling efficient analysis of signals, convolution, filtering, and the solution of certain differential equations. The part emphasizes both the mathematical structure of these transforms and the considerations involved in implementing them accurately and efficiently in Rust.</em>
