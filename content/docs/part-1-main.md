<em>Part I of "NCVR - Numerical Computing via Rust" establishes the conceptual and practical foundations on which the rest of the book is built. It introduces the discipline of numerical computing, the trade-offs between accuracy, stability, and performance, and the reasons Rust is a compelling language for reliable scientific software. This opening part orients the reader toward thinking carefully about how numbers are represented and manipulated by machines, and how the guarantees of a modern systems language can be brought to bear on numerical work. It sets the tone and vocabulary that later parts assume, preparing readers to engage confidently with the algorithms and applications that follow.</em>
